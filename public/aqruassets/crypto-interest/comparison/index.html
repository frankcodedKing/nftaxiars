Bitcoin

Bitcoin is a digital currency and a payment system, first introduced in 2008 by an anonymous person, or group of people, under the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto. Bitcoins are generated as a reward for a process known as mining. You can trade them for other currencies, products, and services. As of 2022, over 15,000 businesses now accept Bitcoin as payment.

The returns from traditional savings accounts are often pitiful, so many people are turning to Cryptocurrencies to improve their earnings potential. Bitcoin, in particular, has been gaining popularity as an investment due to its high potential returns and ease of purchase and storage.

Ethereum

Ethereum is a popular decentralised platform for building applications that use blockchain technology that operates smart contracts: applications that run precisely as programmed without the possibility of fraud or third party interference. Stablecoins

Stablecoins are a type of digital currency designed to maintain a consistent value. They are pegged to a reserve asset, such as gold or USD, and aim to keep price volatility low, making them an attractive investment for more predictable returns.

Why does the interest rate differ across Cryptocurrencies?

The interest rate earned on Cryptocurrencies differs depending on the coin. One of the main factors determining the interest rate we can pass on to our clients is supply and demand.

Crypto lending has grown in popularity in the last few years. Loaning Crypto arises from the relationship between three parties; borrowers, depositors and lending platforms.

Borrowers

Investors looking to loan Crypto for various opportunities. Often, it’s more tax-efficient to borrow against Crypto assets than sell them.

Depositors

Investors looking for opportunities to fund loans in exchange for returns and high yields.

Lending platforms

Platforms who manage these loans and ensure that funds are collateralised, risks are mitigated, and analysis and monitoring are in place.

There is a shortage of stablecoins, compared to other Cryptocurrencies, due to this asset still being in its early stages. An emerging market creates opportunities, leading to high demand for arbitrage and where investors are able to take advantage of these circumstances. This structural shortage of stablecoins, coupled with the increasing demand, creates a market with high funding rates.

In simple terms, the increased demand for stablecoins relative to the number of them in existence results in a higher interest return for those able to lend them.

Which Cryptocurrency offers the best returns?

It is important to remember that the interest rate is not the only factor to consider when choosing a Cryptocurrency in which to invest. You should also think about the volatility of the asset and the growth potential. Some, like Bitcoin, are more established and have a larger market capitalisation. Others, like Ethereum, are newer and considered to be more volatile.

That being said, all Cryptocurrencies have the potential to offer high returns. It depends on the market conditions at the time you make your investment. And the most important thing is to select a coin that you feel comfortable with.
